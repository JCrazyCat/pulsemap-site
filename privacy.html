Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 2026

Overview

PulseMap ("we", "our", "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what information we collect, how we use it, and your rights.

Information We Collect

  • Approximate location while the app is open — used solely to centre the map and improve local busyness accuracy.
  • Anonymous, aggregated crowd-density signals — never linked to your device identity.
  • Crash reports and performance diagnostics via Apple's built-in frameworks.

Information We Do NOT Collect

  • Advertising identifiers (IDFA / IDFV).
  • Precise movement history or location logs.
  • Payment information — all purchases are handled by the App Store.
  • Passwords or authentication credentials.

Optional Local Profile

If you create a local profile (name and optional email), that information is stored only on your device in app preferences and is never transmitted to our servers. You can delete it at any time by signing out or deleting your account in Settings.

How We Use Your Information

Location data is used in real-time to centre the map and bias venue results toward your area. It is never stored on our servers. Aggregated, anonymised crowd signals are used to compute busyness scores for the H3 tile grid.

Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share your data with third parties for advertising or marketing purposes. We may share anonymised, aggregated statistics with partners for research purposes only.

Data Retention

Because we do not collect personal data, there is nothing to delete. Busyness scores are ephemeral and refreshed every 60–120 seconds. Account deletion (Settings → Delete Account) permanently removes any account-tied data within 24 hours.

Children's Privacy

PulseMap is intended for users 17 years of age or older. We do not knowingly collect information from children under 13.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Continued use of the app after a policy update constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
